Hi! I'm a biology and computer science double major in the Class of 2016 at Stanford University.

I do undergraduate research in the lab of Dr. Howard Chang. I primarily study noncoding RNAs and their structure, interactions, and function. We are interested in how these RNAs orchestrate gene regulation, cell fate, and many other processes through noncanonical functions. Previously, I worked with Dr. Long-Cheng Li at UCSF on small RNA-mediated gene activation in the worm C. elegans.

I'm also excited by the role of technology in healthcare and other industries. During summer 2015, I interned at Flatiron Health in New York City as a Data Insights Engineer, where I helped build analytics tools on top of a million-patient clinicogenomic database that will be used for drug discovery and personalized medicine.

In my free time I love running and biking. I follow college football pretty avidly and love learning new skills, whether it's a new instrument, programming language, or sport.
